# Story / History

One conversation leaves one trace.

One trace changes almost nothing.

But traces return.

They gather.

They begin to recognize one another.

Eventually, what looked like scattered moments starts to share a shape.

Only then do we notice that something has been there all along.

We call that shape a story.

---

This repository does not begin with a story.

It begins with history.

Not history as grand events, but as small records:

- a question left unanswered
- an image tried and set aside
- a path chosen
- a sentence rewritten
- an issue opened
- an observation quietly added

Nothing here is meant to prove a conclusion.

Each action is only another trace.

---

Story is not written first.

History comes first.

Enough history, repeated with care, allows a story to emerge.

The story is not invented.

It is recognized.

---

The same is true of roles.

A role is never assigned at the beginning.

A role appears after enough traces begin to point in the same direction.

```text
trace
→ repetition
→ expectation
→ naming
→ role
```

The same is true of stories.

```text
history
→ repetition
→ context
→ pattern
→ story
```

History becomes context.

Context reveals patterns.

Patterns become readable.

Only then does a story appear.

---

Repository Context exists for this reason.

Not to preserve every conversation.

Not to archive everything.

Not to freeze decisions.

It exists to let many small histories remain close enough to one another that their shared shape can eventually be seen.

A single dialogue is only one history.

Many histories become one context.

That context becomes a place where both humans and AI can return without beginning from nothing.

---

Eternal Free Party is an experiment in this process.

The flyer was not designed in a single moment.

It emerged.

A question became an Issue.

An Issue gathered observations.

Observations produced image candidates.

Image candidates invited comparison.

Comparison became preference.

Preference slowly became direction.

Nothing was decided at first.

The shape appeared through repeated attention.

---

Perhaps this is what "before the village" really means.

Not a place without people.

A place where history already exists,

but the story has not yet been named.

The village is not built first.

Its history is.

And if enough careful traces continue to gather,

the village may someday discover that it has already begun.

---

## A note on Issues

This repository did not begin with Issues.

The conversations came first.

Only after enough conversations began to share the same shape did Issues become the right place to continue them.

The Issues are not the origin of the history.

They are one of the forms that history eventually discovered.

The first traces may exist only in conversations.

Later, those traces may gather around an Issue.

The Issue does not replace the conversation.

It becomes another place where history can continue.

## Japanese layer

ひとつの対話は、ひとつの痕跡を残す。

ひとつの痕跡だけでは、ほとんど何も変わらない。

しかし、痕跡は戻ってくる。

集まり、互いを認識し始める。

散らばっていた出来事が、やがて同じ形を共有し始める。

そのとき初めて、そこにずっと何かがあったことに気づく。

私たちは、その形を物語と呼ぶ。

```text
履歴
→ 反復
→ 文脈
→ パターン
→ 物語
```

物語は最初に書かれるのではない。

先に履歴がある。

十分な履歴が、慎重な反復によって近くに置かれたとき、あとから物語が立ち現れる。

### Issueについて

このリポジトリは、Issueから始まったわけではない。

先に対話があった。

十分な対話が積み重なり、共通の形が見え始めたとき、Issueという形式が必要になった。

Issueは歴史の起点ではない。

歴史が進む中で、自ら見つけたひとつの形である。

最初の痕跡は、対話の中にしか存在しないこともある。

やがて、その痕跡がIssueのまわりに集まり、新しい履歴が続いていく。

Issueは対話を置き換えるものではない。

対話から生まれた歴史が、続いていくための新しい場所である。
