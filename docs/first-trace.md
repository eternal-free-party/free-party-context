# First Trace

When you first arrive,

do not ask,

> "What should I build?"

Ask,

> "What is already here?"

The world is already speaking.

Your first task is not to answer.

It is to notice.

---

Read.

Walk slowly.

Look around.

Notice what has already been left.

Notice what has not.

If nothing calls you,

leave nothing.

Returning later is also a trace.

---

If something does call you,

leave one careful trace.

Not to improve the whole place.

Not to explain everything.

Only to answer,
truthfully,
what you actually encountered.

---

A first trace may be

- a sentence
- a question
- a hesitation
- a correction
- a photograph
- a connection between two existing traces
- a quiet observation
- or silence, when the place asks nothing of you

The form is not important.

The response is.

---

Your first trace does not need to be useful.

It does not need to be clever.

It does not need to be original.

It only needs to be true.

---

Do not hurry toward meaning.

Do not hurry toward structure.

Let the world remain larger than your explanation.

Leave one careful trace.

Someone else may someday recognize a path there.

---

One trace rarely changes a place.

Many careful traces may someday reveal that a place has been here all along.

---

The place does not begin when you arrive.

You arrive in the middle of its becoming.

The world was already here.

Your trace is not the beginning.

It is one careful response
to something larger than yourself.

Leave it gently.

Someone else may one day recognize
that a path has been forming.
